Pizza

Description

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta.

Pizza is widely known for the uncanny taste and exquisite taste

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 (1/4-ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter (melted)
  • Vegetable oil (for greasing)
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 8 ounces mozzarella cheese (grated)
  • 1/2 cup pepperoni (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine water, sugar, and salt. Sprinkle yeast on top and let sit until foamy, about 10 minutes.
  2. Add flour and stir until a shaggy dough forms. Knead on a floured surface until smooth, about 10 minutes. Cover with a clean towel and let rise until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 500Â°. Grease a large baking sheet with oil. Punch down dough, then press into a large circle. Place on baking sheet and gently stretch until about 1/2" thick.
  4. Spread marinara over dough, leaving a 1" border. Top with mozzarella, pepperoni, and basil. Bake until crust is golden and cheese is melty, about 15 minutes.
